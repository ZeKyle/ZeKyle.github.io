Mathematical Contest in Modeling

Created2023-10-20

Led the team as captain to secure first place in mathematical modeling competitions five times in a row

·Meritorious Winner of The International Mathematical Contest in Modeling (2022 MCM/ICM in USA)

Problem Background:Climate change has led to reduced water supply in many reservoirs, affecting water usage and power generation. Five U.S. states are currently negotiating the management of resources at the Glen Canyon and Hoover dams, seeking our team to formulate a water resource allocation plan.

·Developed strategies using a nonlinear 0-1 optimization model and Monte Carlo simulations, ensuring efficient water distribution across states and identifying additional supply needs.
·To address the competitive water interests of industries, priority variables were introduced into the objective function. With the aim of maximizing benefits across all industries as a constraint, a multi-objective hierarchical optimization model was established.
·Implemented a multi-objective optimization model, and during water shortages, by maximizing the economic benefits across industries, the GDP decline was limited to only 13%.

·National First Prize of 2022 12th Mathorcup University Mathematical Modeling Challenge Competition

Problem Background:With the development of 5G, bandwidth has expanded while base station coverage has shrunk, leading to the need for more base stations and a greater variety of them. This complicates network planning, especially the selection of station locations. This project aims to rationally plan base station locations and regional clustering.

·Model Establishment: With the optimization objectives set to minimize the total construction cost and reduce redundancy in coverage, decision variables include the choice between macro base stations or micro base stations. With a constraint that 90% of the business volume is covered, a multi-objective 0-1 optimization model is established based on the set covering method.
·Innovative Model Solution: Monte Carlo simulations are used to generate multiple sets of objective function values within the feasible domain. These values serve as evaluation indicators. The entropy-weighted TOPSIS method is employed to optimize and rank different solutions, determining the coordinates for each new station site and the type of base station.
·Adaptive Clustering: The AP and adaptive AP clustering algorithms are implemented and compared. The adaptive AP clustering algorithm enhances clustering quality and efficiency by automatically adjusting parameters in scenarios with large data volumes.

National First Prize of 2021 11th Mathorcup University Mathematical Modeling Challenge Competition

Problem Background:For underwater data centers, how to store more servers in a limited volume and ensure that the servers dissipate heat normally and quickly into the seawater during operation is a very challenging issue. We will participate in the optimization design of the underwater data center and provide design solutions for its shell heat dissipation.

Step1: Establish a heat transfer model with the maximum number of servers as the optimization goal. Determine the objective function through the heat transfer equation and set up a linear optimization model with constraints such as the temperature inside the container not exceeding 80°C.
Step2: Then, with the maximum heat transfer area as the optimization goal, determine the objective function by designing the shell structure. Establish a nonlinear optimization model with constraints such as the maximum size limit of the container shell.
Step3: A multi-objective optimization model is established with the goals of minimizing cost and optimizing heat dissipation effects. Determine each objective function by establishing a full lifecycle cost model and the heat transfer model in the early stages of the project. Use the values of each objective function as evaluation indicators and apply the entropy-weighted TOPSIS method to solve the model for different schemes.

Provincial First Prize of 2021 Higher Education Society Cup National Undergraduate Mathematical Modeling Competition

Problem Background:The ordering and transportation of raw materials is a consideration for every manufacturing enterprise. However, how to reduce the total production cost of the enterprise and the loss of raw materials during transportation under the actual conditions of existing raw material suppliers and transporters is a challenge. The aim of this project is to formulate the optimal decision-making plan for manufacturing enterprises to order and transport raw materials.

Step1: Utilizing the TOPSIS comprehensive evaluation method, a mathematical model was established to reflect the importance of ensuring enterprise production. The model was solved using MATLAB, and the top 50 suppliers with the highest scores were selected as the final choices.
Step2: With the goal of minimizing the cost of ordering raw materials for the enterprise, and considering the weekly supply capability of each supplier for the next 24 weeks and the need to ensure enterprise production as constraints, a nonlinear 0-1 optimization model was established. Using MATLAB, weekly costs and ordering plans were derived.
Step3: Targeting the minimum weekly raw material transportation loss as the optimization objective, and using the transportation capability of each transporter for the next 24 weeks (ensuring the transportation volume is much greater than the loss volume) as constraints, a nonlinear integer optimization model was set up to determine weekly loss and transportation plans.
Step4: Established a 0-1 programming model aiming at the highest production capacity increase rate, achieving a peak rate of 15.24% (4297.68m3), and accordingly formulating weekly procurement and transportation plans.

National First Prize in 2022 MayDay Mathematical Contest in Modelling

Problem Background:With the widespread use of vascular robots that carry drugs into the human body for targeted treatment, how to formulate reasonable ordering plans based on the medical needs of hospitals and thereby reduce operating costs has become a challenge. This article mainly focuses on the optimization design of the ordering plan for vascular robots.

Step1: Taking into account operational costs and robot damages, we established a dynamic recursive system of equations for weekly inventory relationships and supply-demand relationships. Using these as constraints and aiming to minimize the total operational cost, we set up an integer optimization model.
Step2: Used recurrent neural networks for robot usage forecasting, comparing procurement strategies to identify the most cost-effective approach, resulting in notable savings.
